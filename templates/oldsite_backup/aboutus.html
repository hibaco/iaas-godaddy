About Us

IAAS is firmly rooted in international soil, having educated children of Japanese~American families for twenty years. Originally founded in Connecticut as the Greenwich Kokusai Gakuen or Greenwich International School, the Academy expanded to New Jersey fifteen years ago as the Asunaro Kokusai Gakuen, or Acorn Children's Corner. In Japanese, Asunaro means to develop or grow big and strong from a seed. Just as our students have developed, so has our school. Asunaro expanded and evolved through several locations in New Jersey, and it is with great pride that we now occupy a building that has educated young children in Closter and nearby communities for over 50 years. Our new home boasts large, sunny, fully-carpeted classrooms; dedicated music and dance room; large multi-purpose room; and age-appropriate outdoor play area. We neighbor the Closter Public Library; Belskie Art Museum; and a newly developed outdoor nature park.


Together with Asunaro Kokusai, the International Academy of Arts and Science now embraces young students from many countries and cultures and teaches an international curriculum which celebrates all children of the world. At IAAS, diversity is lived and shared every day and it is our differences which unite and bind us. IAAS offers every child a rich multi-cultural experience which truly sets us "a world apart" from other early childhood education centers.
