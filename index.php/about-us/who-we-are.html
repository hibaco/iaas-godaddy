Who We Are

The International Academy of Arts and Sciences is a private, non-profit preschool in Bergen County New Jersey educating children in experiential learning programs from ages 2 1/2 to 6. Our school embraces young students from many countries and cultures and teaches an international curriculum that celebrates all the children of the world. Here, diversity is lived and shared every day as we strive to develop tomorrow's global citizens by "educating children of the world, for the world."

Our Director

Kay Yamamoto, IAAS’s director and founder, was born in Japan and educated in America with a master’s in bilingual education. Kay has been a leader in bilingual education for 20 years, first establishing her educational foundation to teach Japanese, French and Spanish language and culture to over 1000 elementary students in the Greenwich, CT school system. After 5 years of directing and training the teachers to implement this progressive program, Kay decided to respond to the numerous requests to open bilingual preschools in the metropolitan area and opened 2 preschools in 1996 —one in Connecticut and one in New Jersey, known as Asunaro Kokusai. Asunaro is the name of a cypress tree whose literal translation is “to become,” and kokusai meaning “international.” In 2010, the name of the school was officially changed to the International Academy of Arts and Sciences to fully reflect the school’s mission to continue teaching an international curriculum to a diverse student body.

Kay brings cultural depth and perspective and is passionate about helping our students’ talented and gifted minds develop to their fullest potential. It is our belief that a fine preschool education is even more important than the education one receives in the primary and secondary years, given that the benefits resonate more deeply due to the intellectual and emotional influences of the formative years of early childhood. The children are given the freedom to play, explore and experiment, yet are afforded the structure to develop writing, mathematics, and languages in a friendly, positive atmosphere so that they are prepared for a successful transition into public school programs.

Our Administration

IAAS is governed by a Board of Trustees. The Board is primarily responsible for carrying out the school's mission and insuring its long term financial, physical and educational viability by providing advice and counsel to the director and other school leaders. Board members are accomplished, distinguished members of the world of education, child psychology, business, law and beyond. They share a keen appreciation for the mission of the School, and, with their worldly perspective, they help assure that IAAS grows and adapts in ways that are tactically sound for the community that the school ultimately serves. The board meets once each year and gathers at other times as needed in smaller groups to address specific issues.

Admission Policy

IAAS admits students of any race, color, national and ethnic origin to all the rights, privileges, programs, and activities generally accorded or made available to students at the school. It does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national and ethnic origin in administration of its educational policies, admissions policies, scholarship and loan programs, and athletic and other school-administered programs.
